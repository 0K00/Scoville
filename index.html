Scoville Scale

Invented in 1912 by pharmacologist Wilbur Scoville, this measuring scale determines the strength of hot peppers and peppers, a strength generally related to their capsaicin content. To establish its ranking, Scoville prepared a chili pepper solution which was tested by five people. As long as the sensation of spiciness subsisted, it increased its dilution. The score on the scale represents the level of dilution necessary for the stinging sensation to disappear completely.
